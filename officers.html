Major: Biochemistry

About me:

I chose to major in Biochemistry because I believed it would culminate my passion for the two subjects I held in equally high interest for in high school. Learning the chemistry behind the biological processes of living organisms has completely enthralled me and I am glad I chose to study Biochemistry.

Coming to UCLA has exposed me to countless faculty research, introduced me to Nobel laureates, and has fully immersed me in the exciting world of science, ultimately solidifying my true love and interest in research in the sciences. Listening to and conversing with passionate experts in their respective fields, who show genuine enthusiasm for even the faintest glimpses of new understanding has influenced me to pursue an M.D/P.h.D. Applying science to clinically relevant, biological problems will be truly rewarding for me and give me a direct outlet to making a difference in the world.

Grace Woo - VP External Affairs
CONTACT
Email: gracethechemist@yahoo.com      
Major: Environmental Science, Concentration in Environmental Engineering
Class of: 2015
About me:

Why I Chose my Major: Preparing for a sustainable future calls for investigating new technologies that can provide for energy, water, and mineral resource demands while minimizing and even reversing environmental damage. I chose my major because I am interested in how science can provide solutions to the environmental problems we face today, and how existing processes can be improved for the future. I am especially fascinated by creative biogeochemical approaches to cleaning up pollution in the environment, namely methods that employ microorganisms and plants (bioremediation and phytoremediation, respectively).

Career Goal: I am planning to attend graduate school to study Environmental Engineering. My research interests include environmental nanotechnology, which holds high potential to solve environmental problems but also poses significant toxicological considerations, and environmental remediation.

Xun (Charlotte) Xia - VP External Affairs
CONTACT
Email: xxtopuniv.lzbp@gmail.com      
Major: Chemistry-Materials Science
Class of: 2016
About me:

Why I Chose my Major: When I was in high school, I found that organic chemistry was really interesting. It wasn't until we were given the chance to draw those intriguing chemical mechanisms that I knew that I was in love with this subject. I would say that chemistry is the foundation of most of the other scientific realms. I also found that organic chemistry is particularly fascinating because we can not only learn various types of materials as chemical products but also different kinds of reactions that may happen to result in the formation of those certain products, thus facilitating our process of making the materials for major usage or official utilities. I love chemistry, and material science because the joy they bring to me is unprecedentedly amazing and thought-provoking. When I chose my major, I thought material science provides tons of resources and myriads of research opportunity which would further enhance my motivation for scientific studies. And so here I am, as a Chemistry-Materials Science major, I am just feeling good about it!

Career Goal: I haven't made up any solid plan, but I think I would start off with research projects and what not. And then I may apply for an institute that demonstrates a considerable amount of knowledge in the realm of material science and how it combines with other physical sciences. Alternatively, I may apply for architecture-material-based-specialization programs and may study or work there. With my special interest in the area of arts and drawing, and specialized skills in material sciences, I can exhibit certain skills in architectural designing and engineering process.
