Seaborg Symposium 2013


Saturday October 26, 2013

Events Begin at 12:00pm

UCLA Campus



The Seaborg Symposium is an event hosted by the UCLA Chemistry and Biochemistry department each year to highlight and award famous and budding scientists and their significant contributions to science. Students have the opportunity to gain experience in presenting research, networking with faculty, professionals and peers and the chance to meet a Nobel laureate.

This year's Seaborg medal recipient is:


Dr. Kendall N. Houk, Ph.D.
Professor of Chemistry
Saul Winstein Chair in Organic Chemistry, UCLA.


How to get involved:

  • Participate in the poster session for a chance to win $700!
    - Abstract submissions are due FRIDAY OCTOBER 18.
    - For more guidelines, please visit: http://www.seaborg.ucla.edupostersession2013.html.
  • VOLUNTEER! 1-2 hour shifts for help at registration desk, set-up and more
  • Participants and volunteers will be invited to atend the evening banquet and awards ceremony (an $80 value) free of charge.
  • If interested please RSVP at https://docs.google.com/forms/d/1uVVlvJYjd-0Bs2qfcXrgcLrWCG4DvXGDiS6fs4iqvgw/viewform by Tuesday October 15 at 11:59pm. We will forward your information to the volunteer coordinator who will provide you with more details.
